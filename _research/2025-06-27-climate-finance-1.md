Abstract: Climate finance is a critical element of the world’s transition to sustainable development, especially in the Global South, yet little research has been done on whether location is an important element in the environmental impact of climate finance initiatives. Using six different data sources from 2000 to 2018, this paper explores whether green bonds (GBs) yield greater carbon dioxide emission equivalent returns in the Global South compared to the Global North countries using fixed effects. The results indicate that the issuance of at least one sovereign GB is associated with 15% more GHG emissions the year after for the Global North. The relationship is less certain in the Global South. These results support the claim that this class of GBs is an inadequate financial instrument to tackle the planet's climate.
